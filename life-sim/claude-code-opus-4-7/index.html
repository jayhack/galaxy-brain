How it works

World. A 64×64 value-noise heightmap carves an island: water below sea level, beach, grass, forest, rock. A shoreline disc plus fog give atmosphere. Sun, moon and sky colours sweep through a 4-minute day/night cycle.

Plants. Grass cells regrow continuously (faster in daylight, faster in lowlands). Visible grass tufts are instanced and scale with the cell's energy. Grazers harvest the cell they stand on.

Animals. Three heritable species:

  • Grazers — eat plants, flee from hunters, graze in herds, seek mates when fed.
  • Hunters — chase grazers, prefer the slowest in their sensor cone, rest when full.
  • Scavengers — track carcasses (from old age, kills, leftover bites). Steal from hunters when bold.

Genome. Each animal carries an 8-gene vector — size, speed, metabolism, senseRange, fertility, aggression, nocturnality, colorHue. Traits drive the render (scale, body colour, silhouette via a tail/horn quad whose length tracks aggression) and the per-tick decisions (energy burn, detection radius, mating threshold, chase/flee gain).

Reproduction. Two same-species animals within 0.6·(senseRange₁ + senseRange₂)/2 with energy above fertility pay a 40 % energy cost to spawn one child. Child genome = midpoint(parents) + gaussian mutation (σ=0.06 per gene, 12 % chance of σ=0.25 "big jump"). Lineage ids are kept for each animal.

Day/night effects. Nocturnal genes (≥ 0.5) boost sense and speed at night, drop them at day. Plants regrow ~2× faster in daylight. Scavengers get a small sight bonus in twilight.

Performance. Animals render through three InstancedMeshes capped per species. A uniform grid (2-unit cells) answers neighbour queries in O(1). At the default speed slider the sim steps 60 × real time; pause or slow to inspect.
